SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization, which is the practice of optimizing a website in order to maximize search engine visibility and increase traffic from search results.
According to Google, more than 200 criteria influence the SEO of a website. These criteria can generally be grouped into three main components: the technical accessibility, content quality and external credibility. Most internet traffic comes from search engines, including Google, Bing and Yahoo. Although social networking sites also play a role on the visibility of your website, search engines are more important as a navigation tool for Internet users. The success of your website depends on its ranking on Google and other search engines. With the help of SEO, Google can recognize the value of your content as worthy of interest and in doing so, it will get better search results. It may seem complicated at first, but we assure you that SEO is easy to understand and can even be fun

Why Should You Care About SEO?

  •  Qualified Traffic : Traffic generated through natural results is highly qualified. Visitors who find your pages from a keyword search usually have a specific need, which must be filled quickly.
  • Low Cost: Compared to advertising alternatives, SEO is considered affordable because you acquire visitors in a natural way, using your content as a marketing vehicle.
  • A Long-Term Investment: Unlike an advertisement, SEO has no expiry date: the efforts you put in place today will help you to develop your visibility and strengthen your positioning in the short, medium and long term.
  • Keep Full Control: A notable advantage of SEO is that it forces you to invest time and effort into your own website, out of any outside control. Unlike Facebook, LinkedIn and other third party platforms, investing in SEO allows you to develop your own assets, without being dependent, or even prisoner, of a platform that dictates the rules of the game according to its own interests. 

A good SEO implies an updated sitemap.xml file containing only active URLs, an optimum loading time, especially on mobile. A "mobile friendly" experience, Optimized content with rich snippets. Ensure that the programming of the site does not block the indexing of the site.

A good SEO implies presenting quality content that meets the expectations of visitors. Developing the depth of content, avoiding creating light pages or too similar. Optimizing your content under the keywords used by your customers. Ensuring that your content answers your users' questions.

A good SEO implies the history and age of the domain name. Receiving incoming links from credible and recognized sites, thematic to his industry. The index of trust of the page linking to your site. Pointing of external links to the internal pages of your site, not just to the home page.
